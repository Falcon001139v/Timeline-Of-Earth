Phanerozoic Eon

541 Million Years Ago To Present

The Phanerozoic Eon represents the most recent eon in Earth's history, characterized by abundant fossil evidence and significant biological diversity. It is divided into three major eras: the Paleozoic, Mesozoic, and Cenozoic. This eon marks the development of complex life forms, including the rise of vertebrates, the evolution of plants, and the emergence of mammals and birds. Each era reflects dramatic shifts in climate, geography, and biotic interactions, shaping the evolution of life as we know it today.

Key Eras of the Phanerozoic Eon

Paleozoic Era - Known for the explosion of marine life and the colonization of land.

Mesozoic Era - The age of dinosaurs and the emergence of mammals and birds.

Cenozoic Era - The current era, marked by the rise of mammals and birds, as well as significant climatic changes.

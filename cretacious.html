Cretaceous Period

145 To 66 Million Years Ago

The Cretaceous Period is renowned for the dominance of dinosaurs, the appearance of flowering plants, and the catastrophic event that led to their extinction.

Major Developments

Dinosaur Diversity: The Cretaceous witnessed the peak of dinosaur diversity, with species such as Tyrannosaurus rex and Triceratops roaming the Earth.

Flowering Plants: The emergence of angiosperms (flowering plants) transformed ecosystems, providing new food sources for herbivorous dinosaurs.

Climate and Environment

Warm Climate: The climate was generally warm, with no polar ice caps, resulting in high sea levels and widespread shallow seas.

Marine Life: Marine reptiles like mosasaurs and plesiosaurs thrived in the oceans, alongside a diverse range of fish and mollusks.

Mass Extinction Event

Chicxulub Impact: The Cretaceous Period ended with a mass extinction event, believed to be caused by a meteor impact in present-day Mexico, leading to the extinction of approximately 75% of all species, including the dinosaurs.

Significance

End of the Dinosaurs: The extinction event paved the way for mammals to diversify and eventually dominate the planet in the subsequent Cenozoic Era.
